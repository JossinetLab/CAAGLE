What is CASTING?

CASTING stands for CAS9 Targets IN Genomes. It is an online tool providing gRNAs sequences for CRISPR-Cas9 experiments in Candida glabrata species.

How does it work?

CASTING's algorithm is based on a set of statements derived from very recent CRISPR-Cas9 studies. In a nutshell, these studies indicate that an efficient guide RNA (gRNA):
has to be followed by a so-called PAM (Protospacer Adjacent Motif) whose canonical sequence is 5'-NGG or 5'-NAG (Jiang et al., 2013)
should not contain any "repeat sequence" (Xie et al., 2014)
should have a GC-content between 35% and 75%

All the gRNAs which follow these statements are then realigned on the whole genome of Candida glabrata with SeqMap, a bioinformatic tool designed to align short sequences very fast. All the alignment sites founded by SeqMap are called "off-target" sites. As these off-targets induce noise in the CRIPR-Cas9 experiment, the lower number of off-targets, the better your experiment will work.

As a last step, we use the gRNA activity predictive model provided by Doench et al. to calculate a score for each gRNA. The closer to 100 this score will be, the more the associated gRNA will be efficient in cutting your region of interest.

How do I use it?

Just select your region of interest, click on "SUBMIT!", and wait for your gRNAs. You can even export them in six different formats including CSV and Excel. You're welcome.
